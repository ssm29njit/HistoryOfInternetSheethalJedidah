History of Internet-Browser Wars

  • Author: Shintu
  • Published in: 24 September, 2020
  • Categories: Browsers, Technology

Web had become part of popular culture in 1995, with Netscape being the number one web browser choice. Internet creators Tim Berners-Lee's colleague found Netscape Corporation which was built on the initial Mosaic browser to establish the first mass-market browser known as the Netscape Navigator. When Netscape was announced to be a public company in the same year, combined with the popularity of world wide web, came the Dotcom boom(see our article here which traces the dotcom bubble to earlier non-Internet origins, however)

Until the boom Microsoft's businness plan was to invest in multi channel interactive cable television. With the success of Netscape, so humungous and impossible to ignore, resulted in the release of Internet Explorer 1.0 as part of Windows 95 package in 1995. Internet Explorer 2.0 was released three months later, and by then the race was on. Both Netscape and Microsoft continuously released new versions of Netscape Navigator (later Netscape Communicator) and Internet Explorer were released at a rapid pace over the following few years. However the race was on to deliver to new and innovative features, and bug fixing took a back sear. Therefore browser wars were a time of unstable browsers and user headaches. In 1997 Netscape still held 72% of the browser market. But in October 1997, Internet Explorer 4.0 was released and changed the game of the browser wars. It was faster and it adopted the W3C's published specifications more faithfully than Netscape Navigator 4.0.

Both companies risked and gambled quiet a lot of money under the premise of browser wars. There are multiple ways popular web browser could earn income, forexample search tool companies would outbid each other in the war of becoming the default search tool for the browser. Another way is by becoming a default bookmark which was preinstalled with the browser. Because web browser contained wide pleothera of information and a massive range of information, the company that controlled this tool could have the maximum wider reach to the general population. They can use this power to influence people to use their product.

Microsoft's strong hold over Netscape can be attributed to one major factor: great deal of cash on hand. Although Netscape controlled nearly 90% of market share and a quiet a lot of public support, the fact that most of its income is from Navigator and its derivatives, not enough cash made it vulnerable.

The actions from the side of Microsoft was intentional to attain monopoly in the market. It was admitted by Microsoft executive in United States V. Microsoft case about their power to manipulate the market. Along with Microsoft monopoly and other bad deicisions by Netscape lead to its demolition by 1998. After the events, America Online bought Netscapes for $4.2 billion. By this time Internet Explorer has succesfully became the dominant browser, with 96% market share of browser use in 2002 was done by Internet Exporer. This was the end of dotcom boom for web browsers. To this day, Microsoft remain one among the dominant presence in browser market, challenged only reccently by the Mozilla Firefox browser.
